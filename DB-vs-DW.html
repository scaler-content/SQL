Where does Rapido's data actually live?

Two very different kinds of storage — for two very different jobs

Every action on Rapido generates data
📱Rider books a trip
New record created
When Ankit taps "Book Bike", Rapido saves who booked, from where, to where, and exactly when it happened
🏍️Captain completes a ride
Record updated
When the captain drops Ankit off, the ride record is updated with distance covered, time taken, and the rating given
📍Live location tracked
Continuous updates
During the ride, the app records the exact pickup point, drop point, and the route the captain actually took
💳Payment confirmed
Transaction saved
When the rider pays via UPI, cash, or wallet — the amount, payment method, and status are stored instantly
▼
All this data flows into one of two places
Two types of storage
📦 Database (DB)
The live, working engine — right now
🍳 Like your kitchen — cooking right now
  • Used while you're booking — real-time
  • Every tap on the app reads or writes here
  • Keeps recent data (days / weeks)
  • Built for speed, not deep analysis
  • If this goes down, the app stops working
VS
🏛️ Data Warehouse (DW)
The historical archive — big picture
📚 Like a logbook — every ride ever done
  • Used for reports, dashboards, decisions
  • Analysts query here — rarely writes new rows
  • Stores years of historical data
  • Built for complex questions at scale
  • If this goes down, only reports are affected
